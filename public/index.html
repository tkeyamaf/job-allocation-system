How CareerBridge Works

CareerBridge helps anyone — regardless of background or experience level — find a career that pays well and brings real satisfaction. Our intelligent matching system uses clear, fair rules to connect you with the right opportunities.

⚙

The Allocation Engine

Every time you're considered for a job, the system runs through a series of checks in order. If any rule is violated, the allocation is blocked and the reason is clearly explained. This ensures fairness, prevents overloading companies, and keeps the process transparent for everyone.

The 5 Rules

🎯

Fit Score Threshold

FIT_THRESHOLD

Only jobs where your fit score is 70 or above are considered. This ensures quality matches and a high success rate for placements.

Example: A score of 65 won't qualify — but improving your profile can push you over the threshold.
📋

Job Allocation Cap

JOB_CAP

Each job accepts a maximum of 12 placements per week to ensure fair distribution. Once full, it becomes temporarily unavailable until the next week.

Example: A popular posting that's full this week will reopen next Monday.
🏢

Company Cap

COMPANY_CAP

Companies receive no more than 30 placements per week across all their jobs. This maintains balance and prevents any single employer from being overwhelmed.

Example: If a company hits 30 this week, try their other roles next week.
📅

Weekly Application Limit

STUDENT_WEEKLY_CAP

Students can apply to up to 5 jobs per week to encourage thoughtful, quality applications rather than mass applying.

Example: New application slots open every Monday at midnight.
⏳

Cooldown Period

COOLDOWN

After applying to a job, a 14-day cooldown applies before you can reapply to the same position. This gives other students a fair chance.

Example: Applied to "Frontend Engineer at Acme" today? You can reapply in 14 days.
